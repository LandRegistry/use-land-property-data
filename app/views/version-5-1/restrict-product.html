The dataset

This data shows registered land and property in England and Wales that has a restrictive covenant.

The data does not give details about what the restrictive covenant is.

What is a restrictive covenant?

A restrictive covenant is a contract between 2 landowners. One land owner promises the other land owner not to carry out certain acts on their own land.

Restrictive covenants usually happen when somebody selling land wishes to restrict what the buyer can do with it. Although in some cases the vendor will agree to restrict his use of the land he is retaining. The obligation to comply with the covenants remain no matter how often the land changes hands on how obsolete the covenants seem to be.

Access the dataset

There are 3 different licence types that describe how you can use the data.

Commercial licence

You intent to use the data to make a product or service to sell to others. You might add or combine data to make your product.

The data costs £5,000 a year.

Contact us if you want this type of licence.

Exploration licence

You want to take the data to see if you can make a product or service to sell. You can put the data in products, services or prototypes and show it to users or customers.

The data is free of charge and you will get access to the data for 3 months.

Contact us if you want this type of licence.

Personal use licence

You want to use this data for research, for personal use or for your work. You will not:

  • show or give this dataset to anyone externally
  • publish the data online
  • use the data in a commercial product or service

You'll need to create an account and agree to the licence to access the data.

The data is free of charge. The licence lasts a year.
