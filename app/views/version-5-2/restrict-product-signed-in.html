The dataset

This data shows registered land and property in England and Wales that has a restrictive covenant.

The data does not give details about what the restrictive covenant is.

What is a restrictive covenant?

A restrictive covenant is a contract between 2 landowners. One landowner promises the other landowner not to carry out certain acts on their own land.

Restrictive covenants usually happen when somebody selling land wishes to restrict what the buyer can do with it. However, sometimes the vendor will agree to restrict their own use of the land they are keeping. The landowner must comply with the covenants no matter how often the land changes hands or how out of date the covenants seem to be.

Access the dataset

The dataset is updated on the 6th working day of the month. We publish the data as .CSV files. You can download the:

  • complete file - this is a complete file of the current data and contains all of the changes the happened during the month
  • change only file - this includes data that has changed since the previous month's release

How you can use the data

There are 3 different licence types that describe how you can use the data.

Commercial licence

A commercial licence allows you to use the data in a product or service that you'll sell. You might add or combine data to make your product.

The data costs £5,000 a year.

Exploration licence

An exploration licence allows you to take the data to see if you can make a product or service to sell. You'll be able to put the data in products, services or prototypes and show it to users or customers.

The data is free of charge and you'll get access to the data for 3 months.

Contact us if you want a commercial or exploratory licence.

What happens next?

When you contact us, we'll check this is the correct licence for your needs. We'll send you the licence to sign and ask you to create an account on our service. You'll need this to access the data.

Once you've returned the licence and paid any fee, we'll approve your access to the dataset and you can download the data.

Private licence

A private licence allows you to use this data for research, for personal use or for your work. You are not allowed to:

  • show or give this dataset to anyone externally
  • publish the data online
  • use the data in a commercial product or service
  • make money from the data

The data is free of charge.

What happens next?

You'll need to agree to the licence to access the data.
