The dataset

This data shows registered land and property in England and Wales that has a lease registered on them. It shows when the lease started and how long it lasts.

What is a registered lease?

A landlord has to register a lease on a property that lasts longer than 7 years. Some leases that are shorter than 7 years are also registered.

Access the dataset

The dataset is updated on the 6th working day of the month. We publish the data as .CSV files. You can download the:

  • complete file - this is a complete file of the current data and contains all of the changes the happened during the month
  • change only file - this includes data that has changed since the previous month's release

How you can use the data

There are 3 different licence types that describe how you can use the data.

Commercial licence

A commercial licence allows you to use the data in a product or service that you'll sell. You might add or combine data to make your product.

The data costs £5,000 a year.

Exploration licence

An exploration licence allows you to take the data to see if you can make a product or service to sell. You'll be able to put the data in products, services or prototypes and show it to users or customers.

The data is free of charge and you'll get access to the data for 3 months. You’ll need to take a commercial licence if you decide to create your product or service after evaluating/exploring the data.

Direct licence

A direct licence allows you to use this data for research, for personal use or for your work. You are not allowed to:

  • show or give this dataset to anyone externally
  • publish the data online
  • use the data in a commercial product or service
  • make money from the data

The data is free of charge.

What happens next?

Commercial or exploration licence

Contact us if you want a commercial or exploratory licence. We'll check this is the correct licence for your needs. We'll send you the licence to sign and ask you to create an account on our service. You'll need this to access the data.

Once you've returned the licence and paid any fee, we'll approve your access to the dataset and you can download the data.

Direct licence

If you want a direct licence, you'll need to agree to the licence online to access the data.
